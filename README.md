## üéØ Step 1: Gather Google Trends Data

### Instructions:

1. **Go to Google Trends:** https://trends.google.com

2. **Set up your search:**
   - In the search bar, type: `AI tools for business`
   - Click on the settings/filter options
   - Set **Location:** India
   - Set **Time Range:** January 1, 2025 - Present (or December 31, 2025 if available)
   - Set **Category:** All categories (or Business/Technology if you want to be more specific)

3. **Download the data:**
   - Scroll down to the "Interest over time" graph
   - Click the download icon (‚Üì) in the top right corner of the graph
   - This will download a CSV file

4. **Explore additional data:**
   - Note the "Interest by subregion" (cities/states in India)
   - Note the "Related topics" and "Related queries" sections
   - Take screenshots if needed for reference

## üìä Step 2: Fill the Google Sheets Template

### Instructions:

1. **Open the provided CSV file:**
   - File: `google_trends_data.csv`

2. **Import your downloaded Google Trends data:**
   - Open the CSV file Google Trends gave you
   - Copy the Interest Score values (the numbers 0-100)
   - Paste them into the `Interest_Score` column in our template

3. **Add notes for significant dates:**
   - In the `Notes` column, add any significant events you find:
     - Example: "Tech conference", "Product launch", "Festival season", etc.

4. **Create additional sheets (optional but recommended):**
   
   **Sheet 2: Regional Interest**
   ```
   City/State, Interest_Score
   [City 1], [Score]
   [City 2], [Score]
   ```

   **Sheet 3: Related Queries**
   ```
   Query, Type, Growth_Percentage
   [Query 1], Rising, [%]
   [Query 2], Top, -
   ```

5. **Upload to Google Sheets:**
   - Go to https://sheets.google.com
   - Click "New" ‚Üí Upload ‚Üí Select your CSV file
   - OR: Create a new sheet and copy-paste the data

6. **Format the Google Sheet:**
   - Make headers bold
   - Apply color coding (green for high values, yellow for medium, red for low)
   - Add a chart/graph of Interest over Time
   - Make it look professional!

## üìù Step 3: Complete the Trend Analysis Document

### Instructions:

1. **Open:** `trend_analysis.md`

2. **Fill in each section with YOUR analysis:**

   - **Section 1: Overall Search Interest Trajectory**
     - Look at your data: Is it going up, down, or fluctuating?
     - Calculate the percentage change from Jan to latest data
     - Example: If Jan = 65 and Dec = 85, growth = ((85-65)/65) √ó 100 = 30.8%

   - **Section 2: Peak Interest Periods**
     - Identify the 2-3 highest spikes in your data
     - Research what happened during those weeks
     - Possible events: 
       - Tech conferences (Google I/O, Microsoft Build)
       - Major AI product launches
       - Government policies
       - Viral AI news stories
       - Business quarter endings

   - **Section 3: Regional Interest**
     - List the top 5 cities/states from Google Trends
     - Analyze WHY these regions are interested
       - Tech hubs? (Bangalore, Hyderabad)
       - Business centers? (Mumbai, Delhi)
       - Emerging markets?

   - **Section 4: Related Queries**
     - Copy the rising and top queries from Google Trends
     - Analyze what they tell you about user intent
       - Are people looking for free tools or enterprise solutions?
       - Specific industries? (e.g., "AI for marketing", "AI for finance")

   - **Section 5: Actionable Recommendations**
     - Based on your data, recommend:
       - WHEN to send emails (align with peak periods)
       - WHERE to target (top cities/regions)
       - WHAT to say (based on related queries)

3. **Be specific and data-driven!** Use actual numbers from your Google Trends data.

- **Solution:** Make sure you saved it as `.html` extension, not `.txt`
